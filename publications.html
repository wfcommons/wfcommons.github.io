Citing WfCommons

When citing WfCommons, please use the following paper. You should also actually read that paper, as it provides a recent and general overview on the framework. 

T. Coleman, H. Casanova, L. Pottier, M. Kaushik, E. Deelman, and R. Ferreira da Silva, "WfCommons: A Framework for Enabling Scientific Workflow Research and Development," arXiv preprint arXiv:2105.14352, 2021.
